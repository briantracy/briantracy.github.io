Books

I like discussing books with people, but I always seem to forget the ones I have read. This list is in rough reverse chronological order

† = in progress, * = favorite
TitleComments
The Fifth Season*, The Obelisk Gate, The Stone Sky A+ world building from the author. I really love how most of the history of the world is a mystery until around the last half of the third book. This book is not afraid to get gritty and realistic, and I think it approaches subjects like death, legacy, sex, and morality in very mature ways.
The Sparrow† (Mary Doria Russell)
Foundation (Asimov) Amazingly gripping story, I am excited to read the rest of the series.
Hyperion **, Fall of Hyperion After reading Dune, I explicitly searched for "sci-fi like dune" and was recommended Hyperion. It is absolutely on the same level as Dune and the storytelling and mystery is great.
Dune Series *** I love the scope of these novels. From Butlerian Jihad -- Reign of House Corrino (10,000 years) -- Life of Muad'dib -- Reign of Leto 2 (3,500 years) -- Scattering (1,500 years) -- Events of Heretics and Chapterhouse, more time is covered that any other book series I have read. I see the series being broken into three parts: The early game (first 3 books), the transition (book 4 - God Emperor), the endgame (books 5,6). Each part had a new set of characters, but had so many references back the the previous books that they all felt connected. This is my favorite book series by far, and I am so excited for the movie in 2020.
East of Eden ** This book had a historical fiction vibe to it that I really liked. Normally, I am not a fan of being beaten over the head with allegory, but I thought that the way the story of Cain and Abel was woven through the entire book was really great.
The Long Walk * First Steven King book I read. This book does a great job of inducing anxiety and making the plight of the characters real.
Declaration of Independence + US Constitution Big fan of the Bill of Rights.
The Slow Regard of Silent Things Not as good as the other two KingKiller books. I am patiently waiting for the third one.
Mistborn Series * My first fantasy series, I really love the convincing worldbuilding.
Shop Class as Soulcraft (Abandoned) Thought the author was a tool.
The Old Man and the Sea I have not read any other books by Hemingway, but I am surprised to see that people consider this his best. I did not think it was that groundbreaking. I might be missing some historical context that would boost this book's meaning.
A Little Life * The only book that I have read that tackles mental health issues in a way that seems genuine. The author, a female, does a great job writing a virtually all male cast.
The Death of Ivan Ilych This is one of those books that I read because I saw it so frequently in book lists. I might have to read it again when I get older to fully appreciate it.
The Catcher in the Rye Not a fan of Holden.
The Tsar of Love and Techno Required reading for my college. I like how each story connected to the others.
The Kite Runner
The Communist Manifesto
1984 I liked this story, but didn't always understand the motivation behind the governments action. Why would they go through all of the trouble of torturing and brainwashing ``problematic people'' when they could just kill them (which they do plenty of).
The Name of the Wind, The Wise Man's Fear * Insane world building. So excited for the third book.

Ender's Game * Classic sci-fi. Really great world building and a twist that blew my mind. One of the few books I have read that 1. deals with kids, and 2. accurately portrays the maturity of children.
Whale Talk Picked this one up randomly and I was blown away by how good it was. Very emotional story that does a great job showing how kids can take part in real life, adult situations.
All Quiet on the Western Front WW1 is often overshadowed by WW2 in popular culture, and I think that's a shame. With director Peter Jackson's ``They Shall Never Grow Old'', WW1 has been given a revival in the movie industry and I hope that opens the door for another film adaptation of this book.
World War Z I wish the movie had been more true to this book, because it was awesome. Interesting note: the author is Mel Brook's son.
The Book Thief Very emotional book with a interesting narration style.
Of Mice and Men Lots of pop culture references to this book (Key and Peele sketch, New Girl (tv show) makes a joke about a character giving an ``Of Mice and Men'' vibe towards a puppy, ...)
The Great Gatsby I thought the movie (2013) did a good job adapting this.
Fahrenheit 451 I really liked this one because of how well it predicted the future (for example, the wife is always wearing headphones).
